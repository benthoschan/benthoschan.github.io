I am a PhD student in the College of Earth, Ocean, and Atmospheric Sciences at Oregon State University pursuing a degree in Ocean Ecology and Biogeochemistry with a minor in Mathematics. I am advised by Drs. Clare E. Reimers (OSU) and Peter Berg (U. Virginia).

Most research on the effects of bottom trawling are focused within the North Atlantic despite the importance of bottom trawling to Oregon and, more generally,  West Coast economies. The West Coast of the US uniquely experiences seasonal changes in storm action and upwelling and upwelling strength that fuels highly productive fisheries. There are few analogous ecosystems from which we can draw conclusions making it imperitive that we gain insight into the role of bottom trawling and its management has played in carbon sequestration alongside the recovery of various groundfish species. *My dissertation is centered on assessing the effects of bottom trawling on carbon storage along the Oregon continental margin using a mixture of empirical measurements to establish a baseline state and numerical modelling to assess its role in the broader California Current Ecosystem.* My work with Dr. Isaac Kaplain as part of the NMFS-SG Fellowship is to enhance the sediment modeling capabilities of the end-to-end ecosystem model, Atlantis, so it can simulate how changes in bottom trawling (including changes due to policies) within the California Current affects key fisheries in the California Current Ecosystem and carbon sequestration.

I received my BSc. at Dalhousie University in Marine Biology and at the University of Guelph in Animal Biology. I conducted my undergraduate thesis project under Dr. Christopher Algar for which I used the ReacTran package in the R programming language to assess the effects of Atlantic salmon aquaculture on the capacity of the local sediment to remove bioavailable nitrogen.

Outside of my doctoral research, I am deeply interested in bioturbation and bioirrigation in marine sediments and modeling these processes and their effects on the biogeochemistry. I am an active member of Nereis Park and co-organize its seminar series alongside Drs Franck Gilbert and Kelly Dorgan.
