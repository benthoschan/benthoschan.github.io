I am a PhD student in the College of Earth, Ocean, and Atmospheric Sciences at Oregon State University pursuing a degree in Ocean Ecology and Biogeochemistry with a minor in Mathematics. I am advised by Drs. Clare E. Reimers (OSU) and Peter Berg (U. Virginia).

The majority of research on the effects of bottom  trawling are focused within the North Atlantic despite the importance of bottom trawling to Oregon and the West Coast fishing industry. The Oregon continental margin and, more generally, the West Coast of the US uniquely experiences seasonal changes in upwelling and upwelling strength that fuels highly productive fisheries and strong winter storms. However, there are few analogous ecosystems from which we can draw conclusions making it imperitive that we gain insight into how bottom trawling and the role its management has played in carbon sequestration alongside the recovery of various groundfish species. My dissertation is centered on assessing the effects of bottom trawling on carbon storage along the Oregon continental margin using a mixture of empirical measurements to establish a baseline state and numerical modelling to assess its role in the broader California Current Ecosystem. My work with Dr. Isaac Kaplain as part of the NMFS-SG Fellowship is to enhance the sediment modeling capabilities of the end-to-end ecosystem model, Atlantis, to simulate changes in bottom trawling within the California Current and how it affects other key fisheries in the California Current Ecosystem.

I received my BSc. at Dalhousie University in Marine Biology and conducted my undergraduate thesis project under Dr. Christopher Algar using ReacTran in the R programming language to assess the effects of Atlantic salmon aquaculture on the capacity of the local sediment to remove bioavailable nitrogen. 

In addition to my doctoral research, I am deeply interested in bioturbation and bioirrigation in marine sediments and modeling these processes and their effects on the local biogeochemistry. I am an active member of Nereis Park and co-organize its annual seminar series alongside Drs Franck Gilbert and Kelly Dorgan.
